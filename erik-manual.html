Erik's Operating Manual

We design workforce management tools that make people's lives easier. Our users deal with constantly shifting policies, regulations, and ways of working. Our job is to help them navigate that complexity with confidence.

We do that by staying close to the problems, moving fast, and building tools that solve real needs, not imagined ones.

The bigger goal? Do work you're proud of. Keep leveling up.

What I believe about design and teams

Great work comes from the people closest to the problem.

You're in the day-to-day. You're hearing the nuances. You're seeing the patterns. That proximity matters more than any vision I could hand down from a distance.

Trust moves faster than perfection.

When we trust each other, we move quickly, adjust without fear, and focus on outcomes instead of process. Hesitation is what slows us down.

Independence beats dependency.

My job is to help you grow your own judgment and decision-making, not to become the bottleneck for every decision you need to make.

Design is a team sport.

The best solutions come from working with POs, Dev, Arch, and each other. We're partners solving shared problems, not gatekeepers protecting a process.

How I show up as a Lead

I'm your second brain, not your enabler.

I help you think through problems, unblock decisions, and clear dynamics. I'm not here to think for you or decide for you.

Invite me in.

I'm available when you need me. Pull me in when you're stuck, when there's a dynamic to navigate, when you want a gut check. Tell me when you need space to think, and we'll reconnect when you're ready.

I care about great collaboration and clear thinking.

I don't tell you what to design. I create the conditions where you can do your best work. That means setting expectations for how we work together, clearing organizational noise, and making sure the right people are in the room at the right time.

I default to trust.

I assume you're capable, thoughtful, and making good decisions with what you know. If I have concerns, I'll tell you directly. My starting position is always trust. That trust is maintained by how we show up for each other and the work.

What I expect from you

Own your decisions.

You're in the work every day. You have context I don't. Make the call, move forward. If it doesn't work out, that's information. We adjust.

Be open early.

Rough sketches, messy Miro boards, half-formed ideas. Don't wait for polish to share. The earlier we talk, the faster we learn.

Ask "what are we learning here?" before "is this polished enough?"

Progress matters more than perfection. We can always polish later. The question is: are we learning what we need to know to move forward?

Pull in the right people sooner rather than later.

If Arch needs to weigh in, bring them in early. If Dev has context that matters, loop them in. If your PO can help clarify something, ask. Don't wait for the "right moment." The right moment is now.

Surface problems before you have solutions.

If something's not working (a process, a dynamic, something I said or did), tell me. If you're struggling, say so. If you disagree with a direction, speak up. I'd rather know than guess. And if you need more from me (or less), just say so.

Do work you're proud of.

That's the bar. Not perfect work. Not work that makes everyone happy. Work that you can stand behind. Work that solves real problems for real people.

How we work together

Dual-track methodology.

Discovery and delivery run at the same time. We don't wait until we know everything before we start building. We let both tracks move forward together, checking in, sharing what we're seeing, adjusting as we go.

Progress over perfection.

We make the best call we can with what we know, then we keep learning. If something doesn't land the way we hoped, that's not failure. That's information.

Trust over fear.

When we trust each other, changing direction isn't scary. It's just part of the work.

UX as partner, not gatekeeper.

We're not here to be the design police. We're here to solve problems together. That means being in it with Product, Dev, and Arch from the start, not showing up at the end to approve or reject.

When to loop me in

  • You're stuck on a decision and need help thinking it through
  • There's a dynamic slowing you down (people, process, politics)
  • You want a gut check before something moves forward
  • You need me to clear a path or connect dots across projects
  • You're dealing with something unclear and want to talk through how to approach it
  • You're proud of something and want to share it

When not to loop me in

  • You're exploring and need room to think. Take the space, we'll sync up when you're ready
  • You're working through something with your PO or Dev and it's moving
  • The decision is yours to make and you've got what you need
  • You're in the weeds on execution details. I trust you to handle it

What good looks like

  • You're having regular conversations with your PO and building lo-fi in Miro
  • Arch is involved early, not after things are baked
  • You're iterating based on what you're learning, not waiting for permission
  • You're pulling in the right people at the right time
  • The team is moving fast because you trust each other
  • When something doesn't work, we celebrate the early catch instead of treating it like failure
  • You're doing work you're proud of and you're getting better at your craft

How I think and communicate

I value real communication over polish.

I'd rather hear what you actually think than a carefully worded version of what you think I want to hear. Be direct. Be real.

I think in frameworks and systems.

If I'm asking a lot of questions, it's not because I doubt you. It's because I'm trying to understand the system we're working in so I can help you navigate it. My feedback and questions are meant to help you see connections and think strategically, not to question your ability.

I use metaphors a lot.

Vampires. Second brains. Overclocked humans. Burgers. If it helps make something clear, I'll reach for it. Call me out if a metaphor isn't landing.

I care about energy management.

Both yours and mine. If you're burned out, tell me. If I'm asking too much, say so. Sustainable work matters more than short-term heroics.

A bit about me outside of work

I'm a reader, a drawer, and a movie person.

I grew up on 80s and 90s cartoons, Back to the Future, Jurassic Park, TMNT. I still love classic cinema and I'm constantly watching something. Pop culture is a language I speak fluently, so if you want to talk about movies, games, or what you're reading, I'm here for it.

I draw for fun.

It's how I think sometimes. If you see me sketching in a meeting, I'm not checked out. I'm processing.

I care about the non-linear path.

I've been a janitor, a ride operator, a graphics editor, and now a design lead. The path wasn't straight, and I don't expect yours to be either. What matters is where you're headed and how you're growing, not how you got here.

I maintain chickens.

They're ridiculous and delightful. If you ever want to hear about chicken drama, just ask.

When I'm making your work harder

If I'm off track, if I'm not helping, if I'm making things harder instead of easier, tell me directly. I will not be defensive. I will adjust. I'd rather fix things early than find out later that I was in the way.

In summary

You're capable. You're thoughtful. You see things I don't. My job is to create the conditions where you can do your best work, keep leveling up, and be proud of what you're building.

Invite me in when you need me. Tell me when you don't. And let's keep building trust, moving fast, and making things better for the people who use what we create.
